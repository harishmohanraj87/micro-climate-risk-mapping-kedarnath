# Micro-Climate Risk Mapping for Himalayan Agriculture

## Project Overview

This research project investigates the use of satellite remote sensing and meteorological data to identify windows of crop-loss vulnerability in the Kedarnath Valley, Uttarakhand. The study aims to support climate-resilient agriculture by developing a framework to assess and visualize microclimate risks affecting farming communities in mountainous regions.

## Research Objectives

- Analyze the relationship between weather variability and agricultural vulnerability.
- Integrate satellite-derived indicators with meteorological observations.
- Develop spatial risk maps for crop-loss assessment.
- Evaluate the influence of elevation and local climatic conditions on agricultural risk.

## Research Questions

1. Which combination of satellite and weather variables best predicts crop-loss events at a micro-climate scale?
2. Can vulnerability windows be forecasted with sufficient lead time to support farmer decision-making?
3. How does agricultural risk vary across different elevation zones within the Kedarnath Valley?

## Data Sources

The project utilizes data from the following sources:

- India Meteorological Department (IMD)
- NASA EarthData (MODIS and Landsat)
- Sentinel-2 (Copernicus Programme)
- ISRO Bhuvan and MOSDAC
- ICAR and State Agriculture Department Records
- Ground-truth observations and crop-loss records

## Methodology

The research workflow consists of:

1. Literature Review and Gap Analysis
2. Data Collection and Preprocessing
3. Feature Engineering and Data Integration
4. Spatial and Statistical Analysis
5. Risk Modeling and Visualization
6. Results Evaluation and Interpretation
7. Conference Paper Preparation
